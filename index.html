FAQ

Questions you
probably already have.

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a disciplined way to invest a fixed amount monthly into mutual funds. It removes the pressure of timing the market, averages your purchase cost over time, and lets compounding build your wealth steadily. Even ₹500/month, started at 25, can grow to lakhs by 50.

Absolutely. SIPs can begin with as little as ₹500 per month. The key is to start now and stay consistent. Starting with ₹1,000/month at age 25 for 30 years (assuming 12% returns) can grow to over ₹35 lakhs — the earlier you start, the more time works for you.

Mutual funds are regulated by SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) and managed by AMFI-registered fund houses. Your investment is held in your name in a demat/folio account — no distributor (including us) can access or move your funds. Market risk exists, but your capital is legally protected from misuse.

Direct plans save the small distributor commission, but you lose personalized guidance, portfolio monitoring, goal-mapping, and someone to call when markets crash. For most investors — especially those starting out — the value of proper advice far outweighs the marginal cost difference. We help you make better decisions that compound over decades.

ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) is a mutual fund eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C. You can invest up to ₹1.5 lakh/year and save up to ₹46,800 in taxes (at 30% tax slab). It has the shortest lock-in (3 years) among all 80C instruments — and invests in equity for higher long-term growth potential.

At least once a year, or whenever your financial situation changes — new job, marriage, baby, home purchase, etc. Over-reviewing and reacting to short-term market movements is one of the biggest mistakes investors make. We handle monitoring for you and alert you only when action is genuinely needed.

Most open-ended mutual funds allow withdrawal anytime (ELSS has a 3-year lock-in). Redemptions are processed within 1–3 business days and credited directly to your bank account. We recommend staying invested per your goal timeline, but liquidity is always available when you genuinely need it.
